Key Findings

Performance Gap: 27% spread between top and bottom reps. Walton Stroup generates 30x his salary in revenue vs. Rikki Fleisher at 19x. The metric isn't revenue alone — it's revenue per salary dollar.
Revenue Distribution: Order totals are right-skewed. Mean exceeds median — a few large orders drive outsized revenue. Built a regression model to predict order revenue from item quantity.
Product Concentration: Specialty Paper drives 39% of revenue. Parchment Stationary Set alone generates $3.59M — more than entire sub-categories.
Predictive Model: Used the regression equation to forecast revenue at 900 units and reverse-solve for quantity at $25K — giving sales reps concrete data-driven targets.

What Makes This Model Different

Three Payment Structures: Factory: 70% cash / 30% credit. Tradeshows: 10% cash / 90% credit. Preorders: 65% credit card / 35% Net-30 invoicing. Each hits cash flow on different timelines.
Seasonal Staggering: Channels peak at different months — tradeshows spike Nov ($39K), factory peaks Dec ($12.9K), preorders peak Dec ($25.3K). This staggering was modeled into the cash flow forecast.
COGS Pressure: Material costs at 37.5% vs. industry 34.3%. Flagged as margin risk and modeled the breakeven impact.

Strategic Analysis

Market Gap: 29,700 pet-owning households with 0.15% mobile grooming penetration. No competitor owns senior pet care or eco-friendly positioning.
Breakeven Math: $7,738/month fixed costs ÷ $47.60 contribution margin = 162 services/month. Achievable at 50% utilization with 2 vans doing 4 pets/day.
Risk Framework: Van breakdown (100% revenue halt → $15K contingency), seasonal drop (30% winter decline → "Winter Wellness" package), shutdown trigger (revenue below 50% of expenses for 3 months).
